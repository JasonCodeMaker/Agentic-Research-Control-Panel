# Guide: Related Work

## Role
Position the paper against prior work by clustering, not cataloguing.

## Move sequence
1. **Category clustering** — group prior work by approach/technique, not chronologically.
2. **Per-category limitation** — for each group: what they do, what they cannot do, how yours differs.
3. **Positioning sentence** — one sentence placing this paper relative to the closest cluster.

## Rules
- Organize by method, not by year.
- Show you understand prior work fully before noting its limits — never dismiss it.
- Citation density: the 3–5 most relevant papers per claim, not 10+.

## Do not
- Write a citation catalogue ("A did X. B did Y. C did Z.") with no synthesis.
- Claim novelty by omission ("no prior work has…") without engaging the closest work.

## Paragraph roles to expect
one paragraph per category: challenge (what the cluster cannot do) → advantage (how this paper differs).
