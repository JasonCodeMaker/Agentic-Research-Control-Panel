# Guide: Introduction

## Role
Motivate the problem, expose the structural gap in prior work, name the key abstraction, and list
contributions as verifiable claims. Written twice (Draft-0 then Final).

## Move sequence
1. **Stakes** — who suffers and why the domain matters (a real problem, not a benchmark gap).
2. **Problem gap** — the *structural* limitation of current approaches ("existing methods assume X,
   which breaks on Y"), not merely a quantitative shortfall.
3. **Key abstraction** — the named concept that captures the insight.
4. **Design intuition** — one paragraph on why the approach follows from the gap.
5. **Contributions** — numbered, claim-first, 3 maximum, each one concrete verifiable claim.
6. **Results preview** — the headline number.
7. **Roadmap** (optional) — a short outline paragraph.

## Rules
- Contributions stated as facts: "We propose X that achieves Y", not "we aim to address Z".
- Every contribution maps to an evaluation subsection.
- Distinguish a structural gap from a quantitative one — papers need structural gaps.

## Do not
- Open with "Deep learning has revolutionized…".
- List 5+ contributions (weakens all of them).
- Promise results not delivered by the end of the paper.

## Paragraph roles to expect
opening (stakes) → challenge (gap) → method (abstraction + intuition) → contributions → evidence (preview).
