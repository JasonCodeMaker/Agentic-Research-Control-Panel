# Guide: Abstract

## Role
Compress the whole paper into 150–250 words: problem → limitation of prior work → proposed method →
key result (with a concrete number) → significance.

## Structure
1. One sentence of stakes / problem.
2. The structural limitation of existing approaches.
3. The proposed method, named.
4. The headline result with a real metric ("achieves 47.3 Recall@5 on MSR-VTT").
5. One sentence of significance (what becomes possible).

## Rules
- Name the method/model.
- Include at least one concrete metric result; numbers come from the claim-evidence map only.
- Past tense for experiments, present tense for the method.
- Every claim here must be `supported` in the claim-evidence map.

## Do not
- "We propose a novel…", "to the best of our knowledge, the first…", "state-of-the-art" as self-description.
- Promise a result the paper does not deliver.

## Paragraph roles to expect
opening (stakes) → challenge (limitation) → method → evidence (result) → significance.
